**Key Differences Between People and Company Collections:**

1. **Personal Information** : People records have name fields (First, Middle, Last, Prefix, Suffix) while company records just have a Name field
2. **Job Context** : People have Title field for their role within the company
3. **Company Relationship** : People records link to companies via "Company" and "Company Id" fields
4. **Contact Details** : People can have up to 4 phone numbers (companies typically have fewer)
5. **Relationship Fields** : People have additional relationship fields like "Primary Contact" and "Secondary Contact" that link to other people

**Important Relationships to Understand:**

* **Company Id** : This links the person to their associated company record in the Company collection
* **People ID fields** (ending in "people ids"): These link to other contact records
* **Company ID fields** (ending in "company ids"): These link to company records
* **Account ID cf_713477** : Often matches the Company Id but may serve different purposes in your workflow

**Common Data Patterns:**

* **Empty vs. Populated Fields** : Not every contact will have all phone numbers or emails filled in
* **Duplicate Information** : You might see phone numbers in both "Phone Number" and "Main Phone cf_699010" fields
* **Geographic Data** : Some contacts have geocoding information (Geo Lat, Geo Lon) which can be used for mapping
* **Boolean Fields** : Use "checked"/"unchecked" format

**Troubleshooting Tips:**

1. **Missing Company Link** : If "Company Id" is empty, the contact isn't associated with any company
2. **Phone Number Format** : Phone numbers may include dashes or be stored as plain digits
3. **Tag Organization** : Tags are comma-separated and help categorize contacts for filtering
4. **Inactive Days** : High numbers indicate contacts that haven't been reached recently

**Key Differences Between Prospects vs. People/Companies:**

1. **Lead Management Fields**: Prospects have specific lead-tracking fields like:

   - Status (lead pipeline stage)
   - Source (where the lead came from)
   - Lead Temperature (hot/warm/cold)
   - Converted fields (tracking conversion to customer)
2. **Conversion Tracking**: Unique to prospects:

   - Converted Opportunity Id
   - Converted Contact Id
   - Converted At (date)
3. **Opportunity Value**: Prospects can have estimated deal value and currency fields
4. **Follow-Up Tracking**: Follow-Up Date field specific to prospect management

**Understanding the Prospect Lifecycle:**

```
Prospect (Lead)  Converted  Contact (Person) + Company
```

When a prospect is converted:

* A new Person record is created (Converted Contact Id points to it)
* May create/link to a Company record
* Optionally creates an Opportunity record (Converted Opportunity Id)
* The prospect record remains but shows conversion data

**Important Fields for Lead Management:**

1. **Status** : Tracks where in your pipeline the lead is (New, Contacted, Qualified, etc.)
2. **Last Contacted + Inactive Days** : Helps identify leads that need follow-up
3. **Lead Temperature** : Qualifies how "hot" the lead is
4. **Source** : Tracks which marketing channels are working
5. **Interaction Count** : Shows level of engagement

**Common Usage Patterns:**

* **New Leads** : Status = "New", no conversion data
* **Active Prospects** : Status varies, Inactive Days is low, high Interaction Count
* **Stale Leads** : High Inactive Days, might need re-engagement
* **Converted Leads** : Has Converted Contact Id and Converted At date filled in

**Troubleshooting Tips:**

1. **Duplicate Fields** : Some data (like phone, email) appears in both standard and custom fields - use whichever your team standardizes on
2. **Empty Status** : All prospects should have a Status; empty Status might indicate data import issues
3. **High Inactive Days** : Consider automated follow-up workflows for leads over 30-60 days
4. **Missing Source** : Try to always capture lead source for ROI tracking

## **OVERVIEW OF COLLECTIONS**

### **Three Main Collections:**

1. **Companies Collection**
   * Represents businesses/organizations
   * Stores account information, order history, and business relationships
   * Primary entity for B2B sales tracking
2. **People Collection**
   * Represents individual contacts
   * Always linked to a Company (via Company Id)
   * Stores personal contact information and role details
3. **Prospects Collection**
   * Represents potential customers (leads)
   * Tracked through sales pipeline before conversion
   * Converts into People and Companies when qualified

## **CUSTOM FIELDS EXPLANATION**

### **What are Custom Fields?**

Custom fields in Copper CRM are identified by the `cf_` prefix followed by a number (e.g., `cf_675914`). These are fields you or your team created to track data specific to your business needs.

### **Why Multiple Similar Fields?**

You'll notice some data appears in multiple places:

* **Standard Field** : `State` (built into Copper)
* **Custom Field** : `State cf_698130` (your team created)

**Common Reasons:**

1. **Data Migration** : During imports, custom fields were created before mapping to standard fields
2. **Field Evolution** : Business needs changed, requiring new field structure
3. **Duplicate Prevention** : Testing new structure without breaking existing workflows
4. **Different Use Cases** : Standard "State" for mailing address, Custom "State cf_698130" for sales territory

### **Recommendation for Your New CRM:**

* **Audit both fields** to determine which contains authoritative data
* **Choose ONE field** as the source of truth
* **Document the decision** so your team knows which to use
* **Migrate data** from deprecated field if needed

---

### **Common Custom Field Patterns:**

#### **Relationship Fields (ending in "people ids" or "company ids")**

```
Primary Contact cf_712756 company ids
Primary Contact cf_712756 company names
```

* Store lists of IDs and names
* Link to other records in the CRM
* Comma-separated values

#### **Paired Value Fields**

```
Total Spent cf_698404  stores numeric value
Value cf_698404  stores currency code (USD, EUR, etc.)
```

#### **Geocoding Fields**

```
Geo Lat cf_724387  Latitude
Geo Lon cf_724388  Longitude  
Geocodio Source cf_724389  Data source
```

## **COMMON PATTERNS & BEST PRACTICES**

### **1. Understanding the Lead-to-Customer Journey**

```
Step 1: PROSPECT created
   (Source: Website Lead, Trade Show, etc.)
Step 2: Lead qualification (Status: New  Contacted  Qualified)
   (Interaction Count increases)
Step 3: CONVERSION happens
   (Converted At date is set)
Step 4: Creates PEOPLE record (Converted Contact Id is set)
  
Step 5: Links to COMPANY (Company Id is set)
  
Step 6: First order placed (First Order Date)
  
Step 7: Ongoing customer relationship
```

---

### **2. Data Integrity Rules**

#### **MUST HAVE Relationships:**

* **Every Person** MUST have a `Company Id` (links to Companies)
* **Every Converted Prospect** MUST have `Converted Contact Id` (links to People)
* **Every Company** SHOULD have at least one Person as `Primary Contact`

#### **Recommended Validations:**

```
 Email fields: validate email format
 Phone fields: standardize format (digits only vs. formatted)
 Currency fields: always paired with currency code field
 Date fields: consistent format (ISO 8601 recommended: YYYY-MM-DD)
 Boolean fields: standardize to true/false instead of checked/unchecked
```

---

### **3. Field Standardization Decisions**

Your new CRM should make these decisions:

| Current Issue                 | Recommendation                                          |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| Two "State" fields            | Choose one, migrate data, deprecate other               |
| Two "Account Type" fields     | Use "Account Type (NEW)" cf_726552, update all records  |
| Phone formatting inconsistent | Strip to digits only, format on display layer           |
| Email in multiple fields      | Use standard Email field, keep Work Email for secondary |
| Tags without structure        | Create controlled vocabulary/categories                 |

---

### **4. Handling Legacy Data**

**Fields that appear system-generated:**

```
Tags: "prospect reload-1750285540722"
Tags: "71k_contact_imports_linked_ra#-1750197013544"
```

These indicate data migration/import batches. For your new CRM:

* Create proper import tracking system
* Use import_id field instead of tags
* Keep audit trail of data sources

---

### **5. Recommended Schema Improvements**

#### **Simplify Relationship Fields:**

Current structure (complex):

```
Primary Contact cf_712756 company ids  "123,456,789"
Primary Contact cf_712756 company names  "ABC Corp,XYZ Inc"
```

Recommended structure (normalized):

```
Create junction table: company_contacts
- company_id
- person_id  
- relationship_type (primary, secondary, billing, etc.)
- created_at
```

#### **Consolidate Communication Fields:**

Instead of:

```
Email, Email 2, Email 3
Phone Number, Phone Number 2, Phone Number 3, Phone Number 4
Work Email, Main Phone
```

Create separate `contact_methods` table:

```
- person_id or company_id
- method_type (email, phone, social)
- method_value (actual email/phone)
- method_label (work, personal, mobile)
- is_primary (boolean)
- created_at
```

- Response time (Last Contacted frequency)

### **8. Query Patterns You'll Need**

Your new CRM should support these common queries:

#### **Find Stale Customers:**

sql

```sql
SELECT*FROM companies  
WHERE active_customer =true
AND days_since_last_order >90
ORDERBY total_spent DESC
```

#### **Find Cold Leads to Re-engage:**

sql

```sql
SELECT*FROM prospects
WHEREstatus='New'  
AND inactive_days >60
AND interaction_count <3
```

#### **Find Best Performing Sales Reps:**

sql

```sql
SELECT owned_by, 
COUNT(*)as total_accounts,
SUM(total_spent)as total_revenue,
AVG(average_order_value)as avg_deal_size
FROM companies
WHERE contact_type ='Current Customer'
GROUPBY owned_by
ORDERBY total_revenue DESC
```

#### **Territory Analysis:**

sql

```sql
SELECT region, 
COUNT(DISTINCT company_id)as unique_customers,
SUM(total_orders)as total_orders,
SUM(total_spent)as total_revenue
FROM companies
WHERE active_customer =true
GROUPBY region
```

## **GLOSSARY OF TERMS**

| Term                         | Definition                                                                                                   |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Collection**         | A group of related records (like a database table). Copper has Companies, People, and Prospects collections. |
| **Custom Field (cf_)** | A field created by your team for specific needs, identified by "cf_" prefix + number                         |
| **Relationship Field** | A field that links to another record (e.g., Company Id links Person to Company)                              |
| **Foreign Key**        | A field that references the primary identifier of another table/collection                                   |
| **Pipeline**           | The series of stages a prospect moves through from lead to customer                                          |
| **Conversion**         | When a prospect becomes a customer (creates Person and Company records)                                      |
| **Inactive Days**      | Number of days since last contact - high values indicate stale relationships                                 |
| **Lead Temperature**   | Qualification of how likely a prospect is to buy (Hot/Warm/Cold)                                             |
| **Account Type**       | Classification of business (Wholesale, Distributor, Retail, etc.)                                            |
| **Organization Level** | Business structure (Independent, Chain, Corporate, Franchise)                                                |
| **Geocoding**          | Converting addresses to latitude/longitude coordinates for mapping                                           |
| **Junction Table**     | A table that connects two other tables in a many-to-many relationship                                        |
| **Normalization**      | Database design principle of reducing data redundancy                                                        |

## **FINAL RECOMMENDATIONS**

### **For Your New CRM Development:**

1. **Simplify the Schema**

   - Remove duplicate fields (choose one State field, one Account Type, etc.)
   - Normalize communication methods into separate table
   - Create proper junction tables for relationships
2. **Add Missing Features**

   - Audit trail: track who changed what and when
   - Computed fields: automate metrics like "Days Since Last Order"
   - Cascading deletes: define what happens when Company is deleted
   - Data validation: enforce required relationships (Person must have Company)
3. **Improve Data Quality**

   - Required field validation
   - Format standardization (phone numbers, dates)
   - Duplicate detection
   - Relationship integrity constraints
4. **Build for Scale**

   - Index frequently queried fields (Owned By, Region, Status)
   - Partition large tables if needed
   - Plan for historical data archiving
   - Consider read replicas for reporting
5. **User Experience**

   - Default values for common fields (Region, Account Type)
   - Autocomplete for related records
   - Bulk actions (assign owner to multiple records)
   - Custom views/filters per user role
